Benefits of the Financial
Planning Program at DU

Digital University’s CFP Board-Registered Program satisfies the coursework
requirement for CFP® certification and has been named among the top
schools for financial planning education by Financial Planning magazine.

Study on your own schedule and at your own pace, with guidance from
Digital University’s esteemed instructors, each of whom is a CFP®
professional.

The online program offers a unique combination of flexibility and personalized
instruction:

  • Rolling admissions. Gain course access within 1 week of registration.

    learning platform with bi-weekly live instructor-led webinars.
  • Facilitator to answer your questions.
  • Meeting the demands of today's financial planning students – providing a
    CFP® education focused on quality and rigor.
  • Choose your pacing – individual 3-month long courses or up to 21-month
    full program access for all 7 courses, with pacing guidance to let you
    customize your education.

    Online CFP Courses

    Boston University’s online Financial Planning Certificate Program consists of seven courses. You can enroll in one of our packages that include all 7 courses over a
    21-month period, or enroll in individual courses with 3 months of access per course. Explore the variety of subject areas required for the CFP Board exam.

    • Introduction to Financial Planning

    • Insurance and Risk Management

    • Investments

    • Tax Planning

    • Retirement Planning and Employee Benefits

    • Estate Planning

    • Capstone Course in Financial Planning

      This course provides a comprehensive examination of
      the general principles of financial planning, professional
      conduct and regulation, and education planning.

    • This course covers 30% of the principle knowledge topics tested on the
      CFP® Certification Examination
    • Gain essential knowledge about the financial planning process and
      construct personal financial statements
    • Learn how to work with clients to set goals and assess risk tolerance

      This course provides students with an understanding of
      risk management and the tools and techniques available
      to minimize exposures to risk.

    • Learn how to conduct an insurance needs analysis for clients and
      evaluate insurance contracts for life, disability, long-term care, and health
      insurance.
    • Explore how insurance rates are developed, what types of contracts are
      available, how to read insurance proposals
    • Learn the difference between property and casulty insurance related to
      homeowners, liability, and auto.

      This course explores the securities market, sources of
      information, risk/return, debt and equities, stocks,and
      bonds.

    • Learn how to evaluate different asset classes for different investment
      objectives.
    • Examine quantitative investment concepts, investment theories and
      strategies, and asset pricing models.
    • Explore a wide variety of investment practices touching on Efficient
      Market Theory (EMT), Time Influence on Valuation, and Futures and
      Option Strategies.

      This course examines taxation for individuals, sole
      proprietorships, partnerships, and corporations.

    • Learn about the tax aspects of investments, insurance, annuities, and
      retirement planning.
    • Identify the likely tax consequences of personal and business financial
      activities and select appropriate and lawful tax-minimizing tactics and
      strategies.

      This course teaches how to conduct a retirement needs
      analysis for individuals and to recognize the key factors
      that affect retirement planning for business owners.

    • Evaluate and compare the characteristics of various retirement plans and
      recommend which plan is appropriate in a given situation.
    • The course covers tax-deferred retirement plans, IRAs, nonqualified
      plans, Social Security, Medicare, Medicaid, distribution strategies,
      taxation of distributions, and regulatory considerations.

      This course provides an introduction to federal gift,
      estate, and generation-skipping transfer taxes.

    • Explore the income-tax effects of gifts and bequests, with particular
      attention to the limitations on income-shifting to family members.
    • Learn the nontax aspects of estate planning, including the estate planning
      process, property ownership, planning for incapacity, and planning for
      business owners.
    • Create successful estate plans by balancing tax and non-tax
      considerations.

      This course integrates financial planning topics learned
      in the previous courses and demonstrates how to apply
      this knowledge to the development of a comprehensive
      financial plan.

    • Learn how to construct a plan according to CFP Board’s Financial
      Planning Practice Standards and client objectives.
    • Students will prepare and orally present (self-recorded video) a
      comprehensive financial plan to the faculty for the final project.
    • Students can choose to complete the Capstone course in a self-study
      format or opt for an instructor-guided cohort version with 9 live webinars
      that are recorded and archived for student reference. Offered 3 times per
      year, the instructor-guided cohort version ends two months before the
      March, July, and November CFP exams.
